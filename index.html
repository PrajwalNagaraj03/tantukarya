Our Heritage

The 16-Step Alchemy

Ajrakh is not merely printing; it is a sacred conversation between wood, river water, metal rust, and natural dyes. Passing through multiple washes of hard-water resist, steam boiling, and sun-drying, each motif emerges with unmatched earthy richness.

  • 01

    Saaj (Preparation)

    The fabric is washed with soda ash and castor oil to open up the cotton pores and prepare it for colors.

  • 02

    Asal (Printing the resist)

    Artisans print precise geometric starch outlines to resist color, using hand-carved wooden blocks.

  • 03

    Indigo & Madder Fermentation

    Fabrics are dipped repeatedly in natural clay fermentation vats to lock in deep blues and organic reds.

Conscious Luxury

The tantukarya Philosophy

"Tantukarya" means the work of threads. We stand as a bridge connecting generational block printers of Kutch to the modern conscious wardrobe. By purchasing a tantukarya garment, you support the conservation of water-resilient handprinting methods and secure fair-wage livelihoods for artisan clusters. No two prints are identical—celebrating the beauty of organic human error.
